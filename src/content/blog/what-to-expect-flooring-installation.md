New floors transform a home, but the day the crew arrives can feel like a mystery if you've never been through it. Knowing what happens, and when, takes the stress out of it. Here's what a professional flooring installation actually looks like from start to finish.

## It starts before install day: the in-home estimate

A good installation begins with an accurate measurement and a look at your subfloor. During an [in-home estimate](/free-estimate/), we measure each room, check what's under your current flooring, and talk through which material fits how you live. This is also when we catch the things that quietly wreck a project if missed, an uneven slab, moisture in a basement, or a subfloor that needs repair before anything new goes down.

You'll leave the estimate with a written quote and a realistic timeline. No surprises on install day is the whole point.

## Material acclimation and ordering

Once you choose your floor, we order the material, and for many products it then needs to acclimate in your home. Wood and some other materials expand and contract as they take on or release moisture, and our climate makes that real. Clark County averages around 42 inches of rain a year, and indoor humidity swings with the seasons. Letting the material sit in the space where it will live lets it adjust to your conditions first, which helps prevent gapping in dry winters and buckling in humid stretches. Skipping this step is one of the most common causes of floor failure, and it's why professional [installation](/flooring-installation/) builds acclimation into the schedule.

## Preparing the room

On install day, the first job isn't laying floor, it's getting the room ready:

- **Furniture is moved.** Our crews handle standard furniture. We ask you to clear fragile items, electronics, valuables, and anything in closets or built-ins beforehand.
- **Old flooring comes out.** Tear-out and haul-away of your existing floor happens next, along with old tack strip, staples, or adhesive.
- **The subfloor gets prepped.** This is the step homeowners never see but always benefit from. We level high and low spots, address any moisture concerns, and make sure the surface is clean, dry, and flat. A floor is only as good as what's under it.

## Installation

With prep done, the new floor goes in using the method that suits the material:

- **Floating floors** like [luxury vinyl plank](/luxury-vinyl-plank/) and [laminate](/laminate-flooring/) click together over an underlayment and aren't fastened to the subfloor.
- **Nail-down or glue-down** methods are used for much [hardwood](/hardwood-flooring/), depending on the product and whether you're over wood or concrete.
- **Tile** is set in mortar, then grouted and, where needed, sealed, which is why tile projects run longer than most.

Expect some noise and dust, and expect the crew to be careful around your walls, trim, and cabinets. Transitions between rooms, thresholds, and trim details get fitted as the floor comes together.

## The final walkthrough

When the floor is down, we walk the space with you. This is your chance to look closely, check seams and transitions, and ask questions about care. We'll clean up, haul away debris, and go over how to maintain your specific floor, the right cleaner, when it's safe for foot traffic, and how to protect it over time.

Timing on that last point depends on the material. Floating floors are usually ready to walk on quickly. Glue-down floors and tile need cure time before traffic or furniture returns. We'll tell you exactly when each area is ready so you don't undo good work by rushing.

## How long the whole thing takes

Most single rooms are a one-to-two-day job. Whole-home installs, tile, and projects with significant subfloor repair take longer. The honest answer is that your timeline depends on your square footage, your material, and your subfloor, which is exactly why we give you a specific estimate up front rather than a generic promise.

## The value of doing it right

A rushed installation over a bad subfloor will look fine for a month and cause problems for years. A professional install, with proper measurement, acclimation, and subfloor prep, is what makes the difference between a floor you replace in a few years and one that lasts.

## Frequently asked questions

**How long does a flooring installation take?**
Most single rooms take one to two days. Whole-home projects, tile, and jobs that need significant subfloor prep take longer. Your installer should give you a realistic timeline during the estimate based on your specific project.

**Do I need to move my furniture before installation?**
Our crews move standard furniture as part of the job. We ask that you clear fragile items, electronics, and valuables ahead of time, and empty closets or built-ins in the rooms being floored.

**Why does flooring need to acclimate before installation?**
Wood and some other materials expand and contract with humidity. Letting them sit in your home before installation lets them adjust to your indoor conditions, which helps prevent gapping or buckling later, an important step in the damp Pacific Northwest.

**Can I walk on my new floor right away?**
It depends on the material. Floating floors like LVP and laminate are usually ready quickly, while tile and glue-down installs need cure time before foot traffic. Your installer will tell you exactly when each area is ready.
